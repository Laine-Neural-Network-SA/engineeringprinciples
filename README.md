# Laine Engineering Principles

Welcome! This document outlines the core principles that guide our engineering culture, decisions, and processes at Laine.

---

### We optimize for people, not technology

The biggest limitation on the success of a company is the productivity and engagement of its people. Whenever we make a decision, we will **always, within reason, prioritize making our people more engaged and productive.**

---

### Sustainability over intensity

We donâ€™t want to get into a foot race between our burn rate and a burnout. We care about keeping our development intensity at a sustainable pace.

Sustainability is not a straight line, however. Our development intensity mirrors a **periodization approach** similar to what is found in sports science, with higher intensity, peak, and recovery blocks.

---

### Collaboration in a remote-friendly environment

Laine is a remote-first company. With engineers living largely distributed, we value collaboration very highly.

* With complex tasks, two engineers will work closely to build it together.
* With shorter tasks (<1 day), we either deliver before the end of the day or add an engineer.
* **We ask for help early and prioritize helping others over our own work.**

---

### Match complexity with business value

We have a strong reluctance to accept complexity. 
We focus on accepting complexity where there is a strong impact on moving our business forward.
